State
State TWO properties of non-metals.

(From 1st UT 25-26, Q2c — 2 marks)
Mark Scheme — 2 marks
Any ONE property: low melting point / low boiling point / brittle / dull surface / poor conductor of heat / poor conductor of electricity / low density [1]
Any ONE different property from above (must not repeat the first) [1]
💡 Lesson: State = give facts only. No explanation. No examples. Just list two distinct properties.
Explain
Explain why the relative atomic size of Group II elements increases down the group.

(1st UT 25-26 Q4b — 1 mark)
Mark Scheme — 1 mark
The number of occupied electron shells in atoms of Group II elements increases down the group, so the atomic size increases [1]
💡 Lesson: Explain = Fact + Reason. Here the fact is "more electron shells" and the reason is "so atomic size increases". Both parts must be in one sentence with a causal link.
Compare
Compare the reactivity of magnesium and calcium in the reaction with dilute hydrochloric acid. Explain your answer.

(1st UT 25-26 Q4c(i) — 2 marks)
Mark Scheme — 2 marks
Calcium is more reactive than magnesium [1]
It is because the reactivity of Group II elements increases down the group [1]
💡 Lesson: Compare = state which is more/less + give the scientific reason. "Calcium is more reactive" (comparison) → "because reactivity increases down Group II" (reason). Both halves needed.
