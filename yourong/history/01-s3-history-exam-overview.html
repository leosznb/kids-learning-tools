2.2 Appeasement Policy 綏靖政策（重點！）

Definition: Appeasement means satisfying an aggressor's demands in order to pacify him. 通過滿足侵略者來安撫他。 Mainly supported by Britain and France.

Key Example — Munich Agreement (1938): Nazi Germany invaded Sudetenland (Czechoslovakia), where 3 million Germans lived. British PM Chamberlain met Hitler in Munich. Britain agreed to return Sudetenland to Germany in exchange for Hitler's promise of no further aggression. Result: Hitler broke his promise — invaded the rest of Czechoslovakia, then Poland (1939).

5 Reasons Why Britain & France Supported Appeasement

ReasonExplanation
(i) Fear of warLesson from WWI — massive casualties. Britain also had economic problems from the Great Depression → couldn't afford military buildup.
(ii) Not militarily/economically readyBoth Britain and France were unprepared for another war.
(iii) Sympathy for GermanyGermany was seen as harshly treated by the Treaty of Versailles (e.g. lost land).
(iv) Germany as buffer stateGermany viewed as "lesser of two evils" — a strong Germany could counter the USSR (communism).
(v) Lack of strong alliesFrance had economic issues; the US pursued isolationism.

Arguments For vs Against Appeasement

✅ For Appeasement
  1. Delayed war — created a short period of peace
  2. Respected nationalism — 3M Germans in Sudetenland "should" return to Germany
  3. Strengthened Nazi Germany — to contain spread of communism
  4. "Buying time" — allowed Britain/France to rebuild economy and prepare for future war
❌ Against Appeasement
  1. Hitler was untrustworthy — he violated the Treaty of Versailles by rearming
  2. Encouraged further aggression — appeasement emboldened Hitler to demand more
  3. Ignored Czechoslovakia's interests — sacrificed a sovereign nation

Consequences — Totalitarian Aggression after Appeasement

CountryAggression
GermanyInvaded Austria (1938) → Sudetenland & Czechoslovakia (1938) → Poland (Sep 1939) → Britain & France declared war
ItalyInvaded Abyssinia (1935) → Albania (1939)
JapanInvaded China (Manchuria, full-scale war) → Southeast Asian countries

2.3 Course of WWII — Key Events Timeline 戰爭進程

Nazi-Soviet Non-aggression Pact (1939): Before invading Poland, Hitler signed a pact with Stalin — both promised not to attack each other and agreed to divide Poland. USSR's motive: gain territory, make Germany an "ally", show dissatisfaction with appeasement. Germany's motive: prevent Soviet intervention in its aggression.

3.2 Japanese Rule — 3 Policy Categories 三大統治政策

Political Policies 政治政策

PolicyDetails
Forced Repatriation 強制遣返Due to food shortage, homeless & unemployed were forced back to China. Population dropped from 1.6M to 0.6M.
"Using Chinese to Rule Chinese" 以華制華Appointed pro-Japanese Chinese elites to handle Chinese affairs. Set up two Chinese councils: "Chinese Representative Council" and "Chinese Cooperative Council".

Economic Policies 經濟政策

PolicyDetails
Food Rationing 糧食配給Limited supply of rice, flour, salt. Free trade banned. → Widespread malnutrition.
Military Notes 軍票Japanese military yen issued; HK dollar banned; forced exchange. Unlimited printing → hyperinflation. After occupation: notes became worthless paper → huge losses for residents.

Social Policies 社會政策

PolicyDetails
Media Control 媒體控制Controlled TV, radio, newspapers, films. Only pro-Japanese messages allowed.
Japanisation 皇民化Forced Japanese language learning; renamed streets (e.g. Des Voeux Road → Showa-dori); introduced Japanese customs & calendar.
Imprisonment of WesternersWesterners confined to concentration camps; suffered hunger & disease.
Social Status ReversalBefore war: Westerners had highest status. During occupation: Westerners had lowest status.
Poor Sanitation 衛生惡劣Roads & drainage damaged; garbage everywhere; epidemics (e.g. cholera) spread.

3.3 End of Occupation 佔領結束

1945: Japan surrendered in WWII → Britain resumed governance of Hong Kong. Sir Mark Young returned as Governor.

4. The Cold War 冷戰

4.1 Definition & Ideological Divide 定義與意識形態分歧（根本原因）

Definition: Post-WWII ideological confrontation between the US and USSR — two superpowers that never directly fought each other militarily. 美蘇兩個超級大國在意識形態上的對立和衝突，但從未直接發生軍事衝突。

AspectCapitalist Bloc (US-led) 資本主義Communist Bloc (USSR-led) 共產主義
Politics 政治Democratic government; multi-party system; allows oppositionTotalitarian government; one-party rule; no opposition allowed
Economy 經濟Free market; respect private property; "small government" (limited intervention)Planned economy; state interest > private interest; "big government" (price controls, intervention)
Society 社會Basic human rights & freedomsLimited rights & freedoms
Foreign Policy 外交Containment — stop spread of communismWorld Revolution — export communism globally

4.2 Causes of the Cold War 冷戰起因

① Ideological Differences
意識形態分歧
  • Capitalism vs Communism — root of all conflict
  • Mutual perception of threat
② Long-Standing Distrust
長期不信任
  • USSR excluded from WWI Paris Peace Conference & League of Nations
  • Appeasement aimed to strengthen Germany to counter USSR
  • Early WWII: USSR left to fight Germany alone
③ USSR's Eastern Europe Expansion
蘇聯東歐擴張
  • During/after WWII: USSR occupied Eastern Europe
  • Installed communist governments → "satellite states"
  • US saw this as aggressive expansion
④ Disputes over Post-War Settlement
戰後安排爭議
  • Poland issue: USSR wanted pro-Soviet government
  • Germany issue: led to division of Europe into capitalist/communist blocs

4.3 Forms of Cold War Conflict 冷戰衝突形式

① Economic competition② Propaganda war③ Arms race (nuclear weapons)
④ Proxy wars (Korea, Vietnam, Chinese Civil War)⑤ Rival military alliances (NATO vs Warsaw Pact)⑥ Espionage
⑦ Lack of communication between blocs

7. Key Concepts Quick Reference 核心概念速查

Collective Security
集體安全
Nations act together to prevent aggression. League of Nations failed to provide this.
Appeasement 綏靖政策
Making concessions to an aggressor to avoid war. Example: Munich Agreement (1938).
Totalitarianism 極權主義
State controls ALL aspects of life. Examples: Nazi Germany, Fascist Italy, Militarist Japan.
Nazi-Soviet Pact
蘇德互不侵犯條約
1939: Hitler & Stalin agreed not to attack each other, divided Poland. Allowed Germany to invade without Soviet interference.
Containment 圍堵政策
US Cold War strategy to stop communism's spread. Truman Doctrine (1947) marked its beginning.
Proxy War 代理人戰爭
Superpowers support opposing sides without directly fighting. Example: Korean War (1950–53).
Détente 緩和
1970s period of reduced US-USSR tensions. SALT agreements limited nuclear arms.
Japanisation 皇民化
Japanese occupation policy: impose Japanese language, culture, names, calendar. Example: Des Voeux Road → Showa-dori.
Military Notes 軍票
Japanese military yen; forced exchange from HK dollar; unlimited printing → hyperinflation → became worthless after war.
Sino-British Joint Declaration 中英聯合聲明
1984: HK returns to China 1 July 1997 under "One Country, Two Systems".
Iron Curtain 鐵幕
Churchill's term for the divide between communist and non-communist Europe.
Denazification 去納粹化
Post-WWII policy to remove Nazi influence from German society, government, and culture.
