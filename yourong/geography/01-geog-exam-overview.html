🎯 Key Points, Difficulties & How to Tackle Them

🔴 High-Weight, High-Difficulty

TopicWhy HardHow to Tackle
5-layer structure + plant adaptations Must know ALL layers (emergent→ground) AND match each plant adaptation to its layer. Mixed up easily in MC. Draw the vertical profile from memory. Label: emergent (buttress roots, >50m) → canopy (dense crowns, epiphytes) → understorey (young trees) → shrub → ground (dark, wet, saprophytes). Drill with comparison table.
Deforestation: linked causes→impacts chain Need to connect specific cause → specific impact with causal language. Vague answers ("destroys environment") lose marks. Memorise 3 chains:
① Cattle ranching → soil compaction → erosion → flooding
② Logging → habitat fragmentation → biodiversity loss → extinction
③ Slash-and-burn → CO₂ release → greenhouse effect → global warming
Use because / leads to / results in connectors.
Rock formation processes Must describe step-by-step with correct terminology. Confusing intrusive (below ground, slow cooling, large crystals) vs extrusive (above ground, fast cooling, small crystals). Use 4-step template:
① Where does magma/lava/sediment come from?
② Where does cooling/compaction happen?
③ How fast? → crystal size?
④ Name the resulting rock + HK example.
Draw a simple flowchart for each rock type.
Essay: causal explanation Weak structure costs marks. Students write scattered facts without logical flow. Conclusion often missing. Essay formula:
Intro (1 sentence): state the relationship
Body (3–4 paragraphs): data point → mechanism → example
Conclusion (1 sentence): summarise + forward-looking statement
Practice with past essays; self-mark against scheme.

🟡 Skill-Based Traps

TrapCommon MistakeFix
Bearing measurement Reading from wrong reference point; confusing whole-circle with reduced bearing Always draw N-S-E-W cross at the FROM point. Measure clockwise from North. Double-check: whole-circle = 0–360°, reduced = N/S + angle + E/W.
6-figure grid reference Wrong order (northing before easting); forgetting to divide the grid square into tenths Mantra: "Along the corridor, then up the stairs" (easting first, then northing). Always count tenths from the bottom-left corner of the grid square.
Curved distance Paper strip slips; forgetting to convert via scale Mark start/end on paper strip with a sharp pencil. Align strip carefully along each curve segment. Apply scale: measured cm × scale factor = actual km.
Line graph drawing Missing axis labels/units; uneven scale; not connecting points correctly Checklist before moving on: ① Title? ② X-axis label + unit? ③ Y-axis label + unit? ④ Even scale? ⑤ Points plotted accurately? ⑥ Line connecting points?
*Complete sentence answers Answering in fragments / bullet points for asterisked Qs Look for * on the question. Then structure: Subject + Verb + Explanation + Example. Never bullet-point for * questions.

Module B · Tropical Rainforests

Textbook: Interactive Geography: Saving Our Rainforests · Units 1–5

Unit 1 — Characteristics of tropical rainforests

Three major rainforest regions: Amazon Basin, Congo Basin, Southeast Asia

Climate: High temperature (annual mean >26°C), high rainfall (>2000mm/year), small annual range (2–4°C), high humidity (>90%), convection rain in afternoons

Five-layered structure (top → bottom):

  1. Emergent layer 露生层 — tallest trees (>50m), straight trunks, buttress roots
  2. Canopy layer 树冠层 — dense continuous crowns, most wildlife
  3. Understorey 幼树层 — young trees waiting for light gaps
  4. Shrub layer 灌木层 — shrubs and small plants
  5. Undergrowth & Ground layer — dark & wet, sparse vegetation, mosses, fungi, saprophytes

Plant adaptations: drip-tip leaves, broad leaves, buttress roots, epiphytes, parasites, climbers, saprophytes

Case Study: Tropical rainforests in China (Xishuangbanna, Hainan)

Unit 2 — Importance of tropical rainforests

[Content placeholder]

  • Carbon sink 碳汇 — absorbs CO₂ via photosynthesis
  • Regulates global climate and water cycle
  • Resources: timber, medicines, food, palm oil, rubber, minerals
  • Indigenous cultures and biodiversity
Unit 3 — Human activities in tropical rainforests

[Content placeholder]

  • Deforestation 毁林 — Brazil lost ~792,000 km² since 1970
  • Commercial logging, illegal logging
  • Cattle ranching, soybean farming, monoculture
  • Shifting cultivation 迁移农业 (slash-and-burn 刀耕火种)
  • Opencast mining, large-scale development (dams, roads)
Unit 4 — Impacts of deforestation

[Content placeholder]

  • Environmental: biodiversity loss, habitat fragmentation, soil erosion, flooding, ocean acidification, climate change feedback
  • Socio-economic: indigenous livelihood threats, food shortage, disease spread
Unit 5 — Measures to save rainforests

[Content placeholder]

  • Government: carbon trading/tax, national parks, afforestation, birth control
  • Business: CSR, timber certification (eco-labels)
  • Individual: buy eco-label products, reduce beef consumption

Challenges: HK's sedimentary rocks have undergone weathering & erosion; complete fossils rarely preserved in small, fragmented rock exposures.
